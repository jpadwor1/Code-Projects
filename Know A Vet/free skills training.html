Whether unemployed, underemployed, or seeking a career change, it’s helpful to know that some organizations offer free skills training to Veterans. Such organizations respect the service and sacrifices made by Veterans, and the traits that make Veterans great additions to civilian work teams. They know the value that military experiences in leadership, technology, missions, planning, and workload management bring to civilian employers. In addition to free skills training, these organizations provide support services, interview training, and job placement help. A few offer ‘earn while you learn’ opportunities. In this article we offer information on 6 programs providing free training to eligible Veterans.

Onward to Opportunity (O2O)
    Free career training program offers:
  • online learning
  • training in Business Management, Information Technology, and Customer Service
  • help with recognized industry certification exams
  • connection to Veteran Service Organizations (VSOs) and private sector companies committed to hiring and training veterans and military spouses
  • assessment tool to help align a training plan with skills, interests and professional goals
  • Available to:
  • Veterans
  • Active-Duty Military
  • National Guard and Reserves
  • Spouses of eligible Veterans, and spouses of all active-duty service members and Selected Reserve members
Refer to the Application page for complete eligibility details.
  • How to Apply:
  • Fill out the Interest Form on the Application An O2O team member will reach out to discuss training options and upcoming cohorts.

  • multiple learning paths that lead to Information Technology roles like:
    • Software Developer
    • Service Engineer
    • Support Engineer
    • IT/Helpdesk
    • Program Manager
  • Online training
  • Access for entry-level applicants as well as those with prior exposure to Information Technology
  • Opportunity to interview for a full-time job at Microsoft or one of their hiring partners
  • Available to:
  • Veterans – Honorable or General Discharge Under Honorable Conditions
  • Active-duty service members
  • Guard/Reserve

Applicants must be able to make a full-time commitment over 17 weeks, and able to commit to the instructional time zone for the cohort duration.

Eligible applicants must be willing to obtain, or already have, pre-requisite certification. For eligible applicants that do not have pre-requisite certification, MSSA provides training material and a voucher for the Pearson VUE ITS certification exam.

Refer to the MSSA-Core-FAQ.pdffor complete eligibility and other details.

How to Apply:
  • Multiple steps, including:
    • Joining a virtual brief
    • Selecting a learning path
    • Preparing an application
    • Satisfying prerequisites

Refer to the Get Started section of the Microsoft Software and Systems Academy page for complete information.

Career Forward

Google Certification program through Hiring Our Heroes, funded by Google, offers:
  • Online training for a variety of Google Career Certificates for jobs in:
    • Project Management
    • Data Analytics
    • UX Design
    • IT Support
    • Python (advanced program)
  • Flexible, self-paced training that can be completed in 3 to 6 months, part-time.
  • No experience necessary
  • Individual and group support
  • Opportunities to engage with fellow learners, program graduates, and professionals in the certificate fields
  • Access to job search support, like interview prep and career workshops
  • Opportunity to connect with ready-to-hire employers
  • Available to:
    • Veterans
    • Transitioning service members within last year of active duty
    • Military spouses
    • Some military caregivers

Applicants must be able to commit to completing the program within 6 months of starting.

  • How to Apply:
    • Complete the online application form.

Drive for 500 CDL Scholarship for Military Veterans

Driver training program launched in collaboration with Indeed, U.S. Chamber of Commerce Foundation’s Hiring Our Heroes, Transforce Group, and Troops Into Transportation offers:
  • Class A Commercial Drivers License training scholarships – currently limited to 500 scholarships – providing:
    • Coverage of all training costs, excluding meals and incidental costs
    • Sll required theory and behind the wheel training
    • Travel and transportation to the training locations
    • Lodging
    • All training materials
    • DOT physical and testing fees
  • Available to:
    • Honorable or General Under Honorable Conditions discharge Military Veteran
    • National Guard or Reservist
    • Military Spouse

Eligible applicants must be at least 18 years of age, pass a required drug test, and meet school admissions requirements.

Helmets To Hardhats (H2H) (earn while you learn)
  • This nationally recognized paid apprenticeship program offers:
    • Training in 14 construction trades
    • No experience required
    • 3 - 5 year programs teaching everything needed to work in a specialized skilled craft
    • Training provided by trade organizations at no cost to Veteran participants
    • Affiliate unions and partner contractors operate more than 1,600 U.S. training centers
  • Available to:
    • Veterans
      • Honorable discharged
      • General under Honorable
      • Officer Discharge
      • Medical
    • Transitioning active-duty service members
    • Current Reserve/National Guard
  • How to Apply:
    • Complete the Career Seeker registration form

Veterans Curation Program (VCP) (earn while you learn)

VCP, created to process at-risk archaeological collections belonging to the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers offers:
  • Employment, vocational training, and technical skills like:
    • Digital photography
    • Database entry
    • Cataloging and photographing artifacts
    • Cleaning, scanning, and re-housing photographs and documents
  • Up to 5 months paid learning
  • Hands-on laboratory experience in archaeological and historical work
  • Multiple learning locations:
    • Alexandria, VA
    • Augusta, GA
    • Louis, MO
    • San Mateo, CA
  • Assistance from VCP staff in creating a professional civilian resume.
  • Available to:
    • All Veterans, with first choice given to Veterans that separated post-9/11.
  • How to Apply:
    • Complete the application form available on the How to Apply page.

Veterans Conservation Corps (VCC)

With its environmental conservation and agricultural community partners, VCC offers:
  • training and certifications
  • paid living allowance
  • opportunities to work on conservation and service projects focused on wildfire protection, recreation enhancement, and habitat restoration
  • structure and purpose while getting outdoors and working with fellow Veterans
  • opportunities to serve full-time for several months
  • possibility of a post-program AmeriCorps Education Award
  • Available to:
    • Veterans up to age 35
  • How to Apply:
    • Reach out to a participating Corps. Participating Corps change with evolving programs and projects.
      • Find participating Corps on the Veterans Conservation Corps site
      • Check the list of more than 140 Corps organizations across the United States
      • check on Facebook for individual Corps opportunities

Free Technology Skills Training For Any Qualifying Individual

Generation USA Training Accelerator Programs

In partnership with Verizon, the Training Accelerator Program offers:
  • Free online training in technical skills and soft skills
  • Local and national programs, including:
    • Cybersecurity Analyst
    • IT Support Specialist
    • Junior Web Developer
    • Junior Full Stack Java Developer
    • Junior Cloud Practitioner
    • Digital Marketing Analyst
    • View the complete program list
  • CompTIA A+ certification (an information Technology industry-recognized credential) available for some programs
  • Help finding full-time employment, internships, apprenticeships, or continuing education

The ‘Which Generation Program’ quiz can help individuals select a program.

  • Available to:
    • Varies by program. Basics include:
      • 18 years or older
      • High school diploma or GED
      • Unemployed, underemployed, or needing to make a career change (*some programs may have income guidelines)
      • Highly motivated to learn new skills and advance a career
      • Live within the United States (*some programs may require living within commuting distance of the Generation location)
      • Able to fully participate and commit to the entire duration of the training program
  • How to Apply:
    • Fill out and submit the application on any of the program pages.
